Why George Street is an Urban Transformation

  • George Street 2020 aims to make travel throughout the city; safer, more efficient, and an overall more comfortable environment for people within the city.

  • The pedestrianisation of Gerorge Street will improve local store sales, and overall improving the local economy.

The Transformation

The $2.1 billion strategy began in construction in 2015 and plans to be finished in 2020. The strategy aims to make George Street an iconic street that is a "thriving buisness and retail environment" (1). The vision is to reduce congestion, noise and to create a safer and more comfortable environment. "When the light rail project is complete in five years, George Street will feature a one-kilometre pedestrianised area between Bathurst and Hunter streets" (1).

-

Light Rail 'Spine'

The implementation of the light rail "spine aims to create greater connectivity within Sydney. The light rail will connect 3 main squares creating easy navigation throughout the city. To coordinate other forms of transport, 'TFNSW' is devloping plans to change bus and road traffic networks to intergrate with the light rail. This will maintain eficiency within the heart of the city.

Pedestrian Focus

The vision is to create a street that is closed to traffic, making it a thriving pedestrian environment. This will reduce noise and congestion in the area, making an environment more confortable to be in.



Transformation

The street design of george street will take on a major transforation. The plan also introduces trees to the area to create shade and a greener atmosphere. The pedestrian area will support retail and local economy. The pedestrian street will include outdoor activities such as; outdoor dining, creative and cultural activies, to create a more vibrate environment.

Light Rail Conopy

The light rail stop will incorporate a canopy, to provide shelter for for up to 100 people (1 thrid of a full light rail vehicle). The design will complement building awnings, street trees within the city.

Progress

After the first two years of construction the first section of the pedestrian light rail strip had finally opened between King and Park street (2).

News Articles

Assessing the language used in News and Article reviews, can give information and an indication about different responses to the new transformation. The '9News' quoted that the construction has caused disruption and has caused "strain on dozens of businesses in the area and the personal impact has devastating" (3). The light rail was originally scheduled to be finished by the end of 2018 and opened to passengers by march 2019. However at the current rate of construction it has been rescheduled to open by the end of 2020.

Arguments about the Transformation

-

Store Owners

While no one was opposed to the idea of the transformation, there has been negative feedback from store owners, complaing about the constrction. Due to the construction many roads have been closed off, reducing sales for many store owners. Sydney Morning Herald interviewed an owner of a store along george street who stated that "the proft has gone down most days to a quarter, when they started (construction on) this side, it went down to half but now it's a quarter," Mrs Wu said (4). Many store owners have been struggling with the reduction of sales due to construction.

However, once the first strip was completed and opened to the public, there has been some positive feedback.
